cheapomatone

I made this DIY version of the Otamatone while experimenting with the 555 timer IC. After successfuly making a LED blink, I figured I could make something that could produce a sound (which is like blinking, but faster).

I ended up with this silly looking contraption, which I made using components and tools I had on hand.

Speaker

The sound is produced by a small 8 ohm speaker scavenged from a kid's tablet. I tried gluing it in place, but as it turned out aiming a hot glue gun inside a tennis ball is quite difficult.

Circuit

Driving the speaker is a 555 tone generator circuit powered by a 9V battery.

It generates a square wave whose frequency can be modulated by a light-dependant resistor (LDR). The circuit is turned on by shorting the two J1 header pins.

Pitch control

I borrowed the slider design from Dino's "slider synth". It uses a tube made out of black cardstock wrapped in electrical tape, with the LDR hot-glued at one end. A white LED is taped to a piece of steel wire which can slide up and down the tube, like a slide whistle.

Issues

As one might expect, this is far from perfect, and there are a couple of ways this design could be improved:

  • Right now the circuit is turned on and off using a jumper. While it is possible to connect a push button to the header pins, I haven't found a convenient way to press it while playing.
  • Unsuprisingly the pitch is quite hard to get right. While this is kind of "by design", it is certainly possible to make the slider more reliable.
